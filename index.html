01 — Introduction

What is Ekal Seva Kendra?

Ekal Seva Kendra (ESK) literally means "Single Service Center." It is a citizen-centric e-governance model designed to provide multiple government services through one access point — eliminating the need to visit multiple offices.

Implemented in Haryana, India under the National e-Governance Plan (NeGP), ESK emerged through initiatives like e-DISHA and Common Service Centres to address the deep inefficiencies of traditional public service delivery.

At its core, ESK represents a fundamental shift: from department-centric administration to citizen-centric governance — making it one of the strongest practical examples of G2C e-governance.

03 — Process Flow

How ESK Works

A simple 7-step digital routing workflow replaces fragmented multi-office visits.

1

Citizen Visits ESK

The citizen arrives at the single-window service center — no need to identify the right department first.

2

Submits Application

One unified form. One submission. All required information is captured at a single counter.

3

Staff Digitizes Data

Center staff verify documents and enter the citizen's request into the digital system accurately.

4

System Routes Request

The digital system automatically routes the application to the relevant government department — no manual file movement.

5

Department Processes

The concerned department reviews, verifies, and approves or acts on the request digitally.

6

Output Returned

The approved service output is routed back through the same ESK center — closed-loop delivery.

7

Citizen Receives Service ✓

The citizen collects their completed service from the same center they started at. Physical file movement → digital workflow movement.

04 — Outcomes & Impact

Measurable Governance Results

ESK was not just a digital project — it produced real, quantifiable improvements in public service delivery.

49,627

Certificates issued electronically in March 2015 alone

That equals approximately 1,600+ certificates per day and 67+ per hour — proving ESK handled real, high-volume citizen transactions at scale.

⚙️

Efficiency

Services became electronically issued. Paper dependency, repeated paperwork, and file movement all significantly reduced.

⏱️

Speed

Workflows became time-bound. Many services became same-day. Digital routing removed delays caused by manual file handling.

🔒

Anti-Corruption

Middlemen dependence reduced. Digital logs and fixed counters limited discretion. Trackable workflows reduced manipulation.

💰

Self-Sustaining

Operated on service fees with low recurring government dependency — making it financially practical for long-term deployment.

👷

Employment

Created local ICT-based jobs — district-level service operators, digital support roles, and local service capacity.

😊

Citizen Satisfaction

Post-implementation studies showed improved accessibility, transparency, convenience, and trust in public service delivery.

05 — Nepal Relevance

ESK & Nepal's E-Governance Journey

Nepal is increasingly adopting the same single-window citizen service philosophy — making ESK directly applicable.

Nepal's Digital Platform

Nagarik App — What Nepal Has Today
  • PAN registration online
  • Police clearance certificates
  • National ID card integration
  • Passport and voter card linkage
  • Driving license linkage
  • Tax and transport services
  • Digital local governance reforms

ESK Model Comparison

ESK vs. Nagarik App
  • Type: Physical + Digital vs. Mobile-First Digital
  • Access: Walk-in center vs. Smartphone App
  • Assistance: Staff-assisted vs. Self-service
  • Rural Reach: Physical centers vs. Internet required
  • Delivery: In-person pickup vs. Digital output
  • Audience: All citizens vs. Digitally capable
⚠️

The Gap Nepal Must Bridge

Nepal has built the digital access layer (Nagarik App) but still lacks enough assisted physical one-window service centers — especially for rural citizens, elderly users, low-literacy populations, and digitally excluded groups. Nepal has the platform; it now needs the delivery structure.

06 — Lessons Learned

What ESK Teaches Us

Six key governance lessons that apply beyond Haryana — and directly to Nepal.

L1

Redesign, Not Just Digitize

Success came from re-engineering the service delivery process around the citizen, not just putting existing forms online.

L2

Assisted Access Matters

Low digital literacy and rural geography require physical centers with trained staff — technology alone cannot bridge the access gap.

L3

Integration Creates Value

Connecting multiple departments into one backend system multiplies efficiency and exponentially reduces citizen burden.

L4

Accountability Through Digitization

Digital logs, trackable workflows, and standardized processes reduced discretion and created automatic accountability.

L5

Scale for Real Impact

ESK worked because it scaled to 117+ centers covering 100% of rural Haryana. Partial rollouts fail to deliver systemic change.

L6

Self-Sustainability Ensures Longevity

The service-fee model reduced dependence on recurring government funding, making ESK financially viable and replicable.

07 — Conclusion

The Bigger Picture

"Ekal Seva Kendra proved that successful e-governance is not just about digitizing government. It is about redesigning governance around citizens."
  • ✦ Fewer service touchpoints — citizens visit once, not many offices
  • ✦ Faster delivery — time-bound, predictable digital workflows
  • ✦ Lower citizen cost — reduced effort, no middlemen
  • ✦ Reduced corruption — digital logs and accountability
  • ✦ Improved transparency — fully trackable applications
  • ✦ Better administrative efficiency and citizen satisfaction
