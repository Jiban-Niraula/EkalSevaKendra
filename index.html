01 — Introduction

What is Ekal
Seva Kendra?

Ekal Seva Kendra (ESK) literally means "Single Service Center." It consolidates multiple government services into one seamless access point — eliminating the burden of visiting multiple offices.

Implemented in Haryana, India under the National e-Governance Plan (NeGP), ESK emerged through e-DISHA and Common Service Centres (CSCs) to solve the deep inefficiencies of traditional public administration.

Its most profound contribution was not digitization itself — but the complete redesign of service delivery around the citizen rather than the government department.

03 — Process Flow

How ESK Works

Seven steps replace a system that once required citizens to navigate multiple offices, queues, and bureaucracies.

01

Citizen Visits ESK

The citizen arrives at the single-window center. No pre-identification of the right department required. One door for all services.

02

Submits Application

One unified form. One submission. All required information captured at a single counter — no repeat visits, no duplicate paperwork.

03

Staff Digitizes Data

Trained center staff verify documents and enter the request into the digital system — removing the bottleneck of manual file creation.

04

System Routes the Request

The digital platform automatically routes the application to the relevant department. No manual file movement. No human discretion in routing.

KEY TRANSFORMATION POINT
05

Department Processes

The concerned department reviews, verifies, and acts on the digital request. Workflows are standardized and time-bound.

06

Output Returned to Center

The approved service output is routed back through the same ESK center — creating a closed-loop, traceable delivery system.

✓

Citizen Receives Service

The citizen collects their service from the same center they started at. Physical file movement became digital workflow movement.

The Gap Nepal Must Bridge

Nepal has built the digital access layer (Nagarik App) but still lacks enough assisted physical one-window service centers — especially for rural citizens, elderly users, low-literacy populations, and digitally excluded groups. Nepal has the platform; it now needs the delivery structure.

06 — Lessons Learned

What ESK Teaches Us

Six governance insights applicable beyond Haryana — and directly to Nepal's e-governance roadmap.

L — 01

Redesign, Not Just Digitize

ESK succeeded by completely re-engineering service delivery around the citizen — not by digitizing existing departmental processes.

L — 02

Assisted Access Matters

Low digital literacy and rural geography demand physical centers with trained staff. Technology alone cannot bridge the access gap.

L — 03

Integration Multiplies Value

Connecting multiple departments into one backend system multiplies efficiency and exponentially reduces the burden placed on citizens.

L — 04

Digital = Built-in Accountability

Digital logs, trackable workflows, and standardized processes reduce human discretion and create automatic accountability mechanisms.

L — 05

Scale for Systemic Change

ESK worked because it reached 117+ centers and 100% rural coverage. Partial rollouts fail to deliver the systemic change needed.

L — 06

Self-Sustainability = Longevity

The service-fee model reduced dependence on recurring government funding — making ESK financially viable and replicable.

07 — Conclusion

The Bigger Picture

"Ekal Seva Kendra proved that successful e-governance is not just about digitizing government. It is about redesigning governance around citizens."
  • ✦Fewer service touchpoints — citizens visit once, not many offices
  • ✦Faster delivery — time-bound, predictable digital workflows
  • ✦Lower citizen cost — reduced effort, no middlemen fees
  • ✦Reduced corruption — digital logs and enforced accountability
  • ✦Improved transparency — fully trackable applications
  • ✦Better efficiency and measurable citizen satisfaction
